Revisiting the Baggage Reclaim System

Redesigning the information environment at baggage reclaim to reduce uncertainty and frustration during the waiting stage - without changing how baggage handling actually works.

Type Independent
Methods Survey, Qualitative interviews, Heuristic evaluation, Journey mapping
Output Information system design proposal
  • Overview
  • User Research
  • Journey Map
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Final Design
  • Reflection

Overview

This project explores ways to improve the passenger experience during baggage reclaim, focusing on reducing uncertainty and frustration while waiting for luggage. Through a heuristic evaluation of the current system and a survey of passenger experiences, key pain points were identified - including lack of feedback on unloading progress, poor crowd management, and unclear guidance for delayed bags.

The design proposes a coordinated information system: staff can update the unloading phase via a simple interface, and passengers receive clear, real-time updates on displays, including historical averages and guidance for delayed baggage. By redesigning the flow of information and the waiting environment, the project aims to make baggage reclaim more predictable, transparent, and less stressful, without altering airport operations.

User Research

To understand the challenges passengers face during baggage reclaim, I conducted a mixed-methods study combining a survey and qualitative interviews. The goal was to capture both quantitative measures of frustration and personal experiences, identifying where uncertainty, waiting, and physical effort create stress.

Participants reflected on each stage of baggage reclaim, from locating the correct carousel to retrieving their bags. Survey responses included Likert-scale ratings of frustration, while interviews captured detailed descriptions of pain points and coping strategies. Common issues included uncertainty about bag arrival, lack of guidance on delays, physical difficulty lifting heavy luggage, and crowding at the carousel.

Journey Map

Mapping the passenger journey from arrivals through to collecting bags revealed five distinct stages, each with its own failure modes. Finding the correct belt is hampered by incorrect airline announcements and no pre-arrival information. At the carousel, passengers have no transparency about unloading status or wait times. Identifying bags among similar-looking luggage causes anxiety, particularly around the risk of someone else taking your bag. Lifting bags from a crowded belt creates physical strain and safety concerns. And when bags don't arrive, passengers are left with no guidance on who to contact or what to do next.

Heuristic Evaluation

The heuristic evaluation highlighted multiple pain points, but visibility during the waiting stage stood out as the most critical. Passengers experience uncertainty, lack of status updates, crowding, and physical strain during this stage. By targeting this phase, the design can have the greatest impact on user experience without needing to change airport operations or baggage handling logistics.

Six heuristics were evaluated, with visibility of system status scoring the highest severity (5/5): passengers have no clear information about what stage of unloading is happening. Match between system and real world (4/5), recognition rather than recall (4/5), and error prevention (4/5) also scored high. These findings directly informed the design requirements.

Final Design

The final design proposes an information and spatial support system focused specifically on the waiting stage. A central carousel display provides live, phase-based updates on unloading progress, alongside contextual information drawn from previous similar flights to help set realistic expectations. Clear messaging is introduced for prolonged waits, guiding passengers on next steps if a bag has not arrived.

The physical waiting area is also subtly restructured. A marked standing zone set back from the carousel encourages passengers to step away from the belt until their bag appears. This improves visibility, reduces crowding, and makes the space safer and easier to share. The system gives airlines and unloaders the freedom to decide the method of unloading and does not add communication overhead to their workflow.

Reflection

The changes proposed to the existing system improve waiting experience through clearer information and better use of space, rather than changes to baggage handling itself. Informational displays communicate the current unloading status live, provide contextual expectations using past unloading times for similar flights, and clearly outline next steps if a bag does not arrive. This reduces uncertainty and repeated guesswork during the wait.

The constraint of not altering airport operations was a productive one. It forced the design to work within existing infrastructure and staff workflows, which made the solution more realistic and immediately deployable than a full system redesign would have been.
